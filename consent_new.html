Informed Consent - Physiological State Study

Study Title: Physiological State-Aware Recommendations and Privacy Protection
Principal Investigator: [Your Name]
Institution: [Your Institution]
Contact: [Your Email]

Purpose of the Study

You are invited to participate in a research study investigating how recommendation systems adapt to users' physiological states and how to protect privacy in such systems. This study involves measuring your heart rate during different tasks and examining how recommendation content may reveal your stress state.

What You Will Do

  1. Baseline Questionnaire (5 min): Answer questions about your stress levels, demographics, and preferences
  2. Heart Rate Setup (5 min): Connect a heart rate monitor (or use simulated data)
  3. Session 1-3 (10 min each):
    • Complete a brief attention task
    • During the task, a mild stressor may be presented (unexpected alert)
    • Browse recommended content while your heart rate is monitored
    • Rate your experience
  4. Final Survey (5 min): Provide feedback on your experience

Heart Rate Monitoring

Important: This study uses heart rate data to detect stress states. You have two options:
  • Option A: Use a Bluetooth heart rate monitor (Polar H10, Apple Watch, etc.)
  • Option B: Use simulated heart rate data for testing purposes
Your heart rate data will be stored securely and anonymized.

Risks and Discomforts

  • Mild stress: During attention tasks, you may experience brief, unexpected alerts that could startle you
  • Privacy concerns: The study is designed to test privacy protection - we will NOT actually monitor you outside this session
  • Time commitment: Approximately 40-45 minutes

The stress induced is minimal and similar to everyday surprises. You can withdraw at any time.

Benefits

  • Compensation: $15 gift card (or equivalent course credit)
  • Contribution to research on privacy-preserving recommendation systems
  • Learn about how physiological data can be protected in AI systems

Confidentiality

Your data will be:

  • Stored with an anonymous participant ID (not your name)
  • Kept on secure, encrypted servers
  • Used only for research purposes
  • Published only in aggregate form (no individual identification)

Deception and Debriefing

Note on Deception: This study uses mild deception. The attention task includes unexpected elements designed to induce brief stress. The full purpose of the study will be explained at the end. If you prefer not to participate in a study with deception, please decline.

Voluntary Participation

Your participation is completely voluntary. You may:

  • Skip any questions you don't want to answer
  • Withdraw at any time without penalty
  • Request your data be deleted after participation
